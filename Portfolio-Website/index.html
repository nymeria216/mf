About my PPL(A)

Have a look on my journey to get the Private Pilote License for aeroplanes

Learning

Gaining a pilot's license involves a significant amount of learning. First, you can’t simply jump into the plane and take off; you must understand how airspace is structured, the regulations that govern flying, and the principles of meteorology. You also need to learn the physics of flight and aviation laws. Each of these areas is crucial for ensuring safety in the air. This comprehensive knowledge not only prepares me for flying but also builds my confidence in my skills. I’m excited to master these essential concepts as I continue my journey to becoming a pilot.

Speaking

After studying extensively, the next step is to learn how to communicate with air traffic controllers. Since I’m pursuing a Private Pilot License (PPL), I will only need the BZF II, which allows me to speak in German to air traffic control. Mastering the necessary radio phrases will be essential for my training and marks an important step in becoming a qualified pilot. Effective communication will help ensure safety and clarity during flights. I am excited to develop this vital skill and gain confidence in my flying abilities.

Flying

The final step in my journey is to step into the plane and learn how to fly these incredible machines. This hands-on experience allows me to apply everything I’ve learned about airspace, regulations, and meteorology. Flying involves not just controlling the aircraft but also developing the skills needed to navigate safely. I’m excited to master flight maneuvers and experience the freedom of being in the air. This is the moment I’ve been working toward, and I can’t wait to take to the skies.
